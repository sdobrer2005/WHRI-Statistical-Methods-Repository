## Interpritation of the results

## Overview

This section presents descriptive statistics and diagnostic tests used to evaluate the distributional properties of the data prior to conducting the independent two-sample t-test. 

For each group (n = 50 per group), three types of output are provided:
- Moments
- Basic Statistical Measures
- Tests for Normality

These summaries support assessment of assumptions required for parametric testing.

---

## 1. Moments

The *Moments* table summarizes key statistical properties of each distribution:

- **Mean**: average value  
- **Standard Deviation / Variance**: measures of variability  
- **Skewness**: degree of symmetry (values near 0 indicate symmetry)  
- **Kurtosis**: tail behavior (values near 0 suggest normal-like distribution)  
- **Standard Error of the Mean**: precision of the estimated mean  

These metrics provide an overall characterization of the data and support evaluation of distributional assumptions.

---

## 2. Basic Statistical Measures

This table provides additional descriptive statistics:

- **Mean, Median, Mode**: measures of central tendency  
- **Range**: difference between minimum and maximum values  
- **Interquartile Range (IQR)**: spread of the middle 50% of observations  
- **Variance and Standard Deviation**: variability  

Comparison of mean and median helps assess symmetry, while IQR and range provide insight into dispersion and potential outliers.

---

## 3. Tests for Normality

Formal statistical tests were used to assess the assumption of normality:

- **Shapiro–Wilk test** (primary test for small to moderate samples)  
- **Kolmogorov–Smirnov test**  
- **Cramer–von Mises test**  
- **Anderson–Darling test**
